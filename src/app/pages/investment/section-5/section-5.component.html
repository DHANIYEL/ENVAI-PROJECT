ii. Prospectus

A prospectus is typically required when issuing security tokens for commercial real estate investments. A prospectus is a formal document that provides detailed information about the investment opportunity, including:

a. The issuer: Who is offering the security tokens?

b. The investment: What is the underlying asset or property?

c. The risks: Potential risks associated with the investment.

d. The financial performance: Historical financial data and future projections.

e. The terms of the offering: Token price, minimum investment, fees, and other terms.

f. The use of proceeds: How will the funds raised from the token sale be used?

The specific requirements for a prospectus can vary depending on the jurisdiction and the type of security token being issued.


However, providing investors with a comprehensive and transparent disclosure document is generally considered a best practice. a. Issuers can build trust with investors and comply with regulatory requirements by providing a detailed prospectus.
