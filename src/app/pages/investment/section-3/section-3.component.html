Rules

d. Terms and Rules of Tokenized Real Estate Investments

i. Token Terms

1. Token Type: This could be an equity token, debt token, or a hybrid. Equity tokens represent ownership in the property, while debt tokens represent a loan to the property owner.

2. Token Value: The value of each token is determined by the overall value of the property and the number of tokens issued.

3. Token Distribution: Tokens are distributed to investors based on their investment amount.

4. Token Transferability: Tokens can be traded on secondary markets, providing liquidity.

ii. Investment Terms

1. Minimum Investment: The minimum amount required to invest in the tokenized property.

2. Hold Period: The minimum period an investor must hold the tokens before selling them.

3. Fees: Fees associated with the investment, including platform fees, management fees, and transaction fees.

4. Risk Factors: Detailed information about the risks involved in the investment, such as market risk, operational risk, and legal risk.

iii. Governance:

1. Smart Contracts: Automated contracts that enforce the terms of the investment, such as dividend distribution and property management.

2. Governance Structure: The decision-making process for the property, including voting rights and dispute resolution mechanisms.

3. Property Management: The entity responsible for managing the property and distributing rental income and proceeds from the sale of the property.

iv. Regulatory Compliance:

1. Security Token Offerings (STOs): Compliance with securities regulations, including KYC/AML procedures and investor accreditation.

2. Jurisdictional Considerations: Understanding the legal and regulatory framework in the jurisdiction where the property is located.
